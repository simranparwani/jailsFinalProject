INTRODUCTION

Overincarceration and discrimination in law enforcement, especially in the United States, have been prominent topics of conversation in the past decade. It is argued that people of color are more likely to be detained by the police, more likely to be arrested, and that Lheir bails are set higher when compared to whiLe individuals. In any given year, approximately 11 million Americans are detained in county jails, and many lack the funds to post bail or pay fines. We tend to focus on federal prison systems, and know little about local jail practices.

ANALYSIS

Objectives

Our group analyzed a set of prison data supplied to us by the Jail Data Initiative at NYU's Public Safety Lab. The data covered the individuals at Cumberland, NJ county jail over the course of over two months. Our goal was to explore issues of race and ethnicity in law enforcement by analyzing specific inmate information from a jail over a span of time. Data analysis is vital in determining the severity and patterns of overincarceration and discrimination in prisons. Therefore, we want to make this subsect of data more easily interpretable for the Prison Data Initiative and for other people interested in this issue.

Method

The variables that we used in our analysis were Skin Tone, Gender, Citizenship Status, Charge, Bail, and Bail Amount. After experimenting with various modes of analysis, we ended up primarily comparing White vs. Non-white, Male vs. Female, Citizen vs. Non-citizen Bail and Bail Amounts. We also found the top charges, bail/no-bail percentages, and bail averages for different demographic groups. To analyze the data, we used pandas and regular expressions. To display our analysis, we used HTML, CSS, Javascript, a Flask app, and Flourish.
